<p><p>The Bone Anchored Hearing Aids (BAHA) market is divided into implantable and non-implantable types. Implantable BAHA devices involve a surgical procedure to implant a titanium screw into the skull, allowing sound vibrations to stimulate the inner ear directly. In contrast, non-implantable devices do not require surgery and typically use a headband or adhesive to secure the hearing aid to the bone, making them more accessible for users who are not candidates for surgery. Each type serves different user needs and preferences.</p></p>
