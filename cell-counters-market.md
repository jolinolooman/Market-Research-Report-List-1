<p><p>The cell counters market is divided into two main types: automated cell counters and manual cell counters. Automated cell counters utilize advanced technology to swiftly and accurately count cells, often incorporating imaging systems and software for data analysis, making them ideal for high-throughput environments. In contrast, manual cell counters require user input for counting cells, typically using a hemocytometer or other tools. While they are more cost-effective for smaller laboratories, they can be time-consuming and less precise compared to their automated counterparts.</p></p>
