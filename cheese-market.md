<p><p>The cheese market is categorized into various types based on texture and moisture content. Soft cheese, known for its creamy texture, is often spreadable, while semi-soft cheese retains some moisture but holds its shape. Medium-hard cheese has a firmer texture and is versatile in cooking, offering a balance of flavor and meltability. Hard cheese, the most aged, is dense and packed with concentrated flavors, making it ideal for grating or snacking. Each type caters to diverse consumer preferences and culinary uses.</p></p>
