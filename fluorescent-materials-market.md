<p><ul><li>Organic Materials</li><li>Inorganic Materials</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fluorescent materials market is primarily divided into organic and inorganic materials. Organic materials, often carbon-based compounds, exhibit fluorescence through chemical structures that allow for light absorption and re-emission. These are widely used in applications like organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs) and dyes. In contrast, inorganic materials, such as phosphors, rely on metal oxides and salts, providing stability and brightness for applications including LEDs and fluorescent lamps. Both types are essential for diverse technological and industrial applications, enhancing visibility and efficiency.</p></p>
