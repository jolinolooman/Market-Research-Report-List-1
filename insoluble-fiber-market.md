<p><p>Insoluble fiber is a type of dietary fiber that does not dissolve in water and is primarily found in foods like whole grains, nuts, seeds, and vegetables. It plays a crucial role in promoting digestive health by aiding in regular bowel movements and preventing constipation. Additionally, insoluble fiber contributes to a feeling of fullness, which can assist in weight management.</p><p>The Insoluble Fiber Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 15.20% during the forecast period, driven by rising health consciousness among consumers and increasing demand for functional foods. The growing awareness of the health benefits associated with dietary fiber, including improved gut health and reduced risk of chronic diseases, is propelling market growth. Furthermore, innovations in food technology and the introduction of new products enriched with insoluble fiber are boosting market dynamics. </p><p>Emerging trends include a shift toward plant-based diets, where insoluble fiber-rich foods are becoming more favored.
