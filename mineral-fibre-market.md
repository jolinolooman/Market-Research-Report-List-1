<p><p>The mineral fibre market comprises several key types, each with unique properties and applications. Glass wool, made from molten glass, is known for its thermal insulation and soundproofing capabilities. Rock wool, derived from basalt, offers enhanced fire resistance and thermal performance. Slag wool, produced from industrial byproducts, provides similar benefits at a lower cost. Glass filaments are used in composites for strength, while ceramic fibres withstand high temperatures, making them ideal for industrial insulation and aerospace applications. Together, they serve diverse sectors from construction to manufacturing.</p></p>
