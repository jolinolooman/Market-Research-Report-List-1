<p><p>The photovoltaic roof tiles market primarily features two types of solar cells: monocrystalline and polycrystalline. Monocrystalline tiles are made from a single crystal structure, offering higher efficiency and a sleek aesthetic, ideal for limited roof space. In contrast, polycrystalline tiles consist of multiple crystal structures, making them generally less efficient but more cost-effective. Both types cater to diverse consumer needs, with monocrystalline appealing to those prioritizing performance and polycrystalline attracting budget-conscious buyers seeking reliable solar energy solutions.</p></p>
