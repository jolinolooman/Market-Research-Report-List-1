<p><p>The purified human proteins market is divided into two main types: native purified human proteins and recombinant purified human proteins. Native purified human proteins are derived directly from human tissues and plasma, preserving their natural structure and function, making them suitable for therapeutic applications. In contrast, recombinant purified human proteins are produced through biotechnology methods using genetically modified organisms, allowing for large-scale production and customization. Both types are essential for research, diagnostics, and therapeutic interventions in various medical fields.</p></p>
