<p><p>The radioactive waste disposal market is categorized into three main types: low-level waste (LLW), medium-level waste (MLW), and high-level waste (HLW). Low-level waste, often generated by medical and industrial processes, requires minimal shielding and is typically disposed of in near-surface facilities. Medium-level waste, which has higher radioactivity, necessitates more robust containment solutions like deep geological formations. High-level waste, primarily from nuclear reactors, demands sophisticated management strategies, including deep geological repositories, due to its long-term radiotoxicity and heat generation.</p></p>
