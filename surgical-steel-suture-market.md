<p><p>The surgical steel suture market is primarily divided into two types: monofilament and multifilament sutures. Monofilament sutures consist of a single strand, offering smooth passage through tissue and reducing the risk of infection; they are less likely to harbor bacteria. In contrast, multifilament sutures are made of multiple strands twisted together, providing greater strength and flexibility, making them easier to handle. Each type has specific applications depending on the surgical procedure and the requirements of tissue healing.</p></p>
