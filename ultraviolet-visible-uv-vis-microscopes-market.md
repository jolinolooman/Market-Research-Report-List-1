Binocular microscopes feature two eyepieces, allowing users to view specimens with both eyes, enhancing depth perception and reducing eye strain. Trinocular microscopes, on the other hand, include an additional port for a camera, facilitating imaging and documentation alongside direct viewing. Both types are essential in various applications, such as material science, biology, and semiconductor inspection, where UV-Vis analysis is crucial for examining sample properties.</p></p>
